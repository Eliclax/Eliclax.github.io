There will be {{ page.duration }} hours of teaching per week every {{ page.time }} over 40 weeks, and I expect students to spend about {{ page.workload }} per week reviewing the material and doing homework. The total time spent in this course should be about 160 hours over the year.  The content focuses on multichoice or short-answer questions which feature on competitions like the AMC Junior/Intermediate, AMC 8/10, Mathex, ICAS, Otago Problem Challenge and Junior Maths Competition, and so on.

## Syllabus
- **Algebra (9 weeks):** algebraic manipulation, linear and quadratic equations, systems of equations, rates and proportions, exponents and radicals, sequences and series, substitutions, statistical measures.
- **Counting (6 weeks):** counting and advanced counting, Venn diagrams, casework, permutations and combinations, stars and bars, Pascal's triangle.
- **Probability (3 weeks):** intro to probability, events, combinatorics, casework, complements, expected value
- **Number Theory (6 weeks):** primes and divisibility, factors, number bases, units digit, fractions and decimals, modular arithmetic
- **Geometry (6 weeks):** intro to geometry, circles, Pythagoras' theorem, area, 3D geometry, similarity.
- **Reviews, Problem Sets, and Tests (10 weeks):** interspersed throughout the year.

## Learning Outcomes
- Use algebra confidently to interpret and solve problems with multiple unknowns.
- Develop an intuitive understanding of linear and quadratic functions and their graphs.
- Employ algebra to solve finite or infinite arithmetic problems, including arithmetic and geometric series.
- Apply the principles of using addition, multiplication, subtraction, and division in counting problems.
- Use permutations and combinations, including stars and bars, to solve more complex counting problems.
- Have a basic understanding of Pascal's triangle and its relationship to combinations.
- Use a variety of probabilistic and combinatorial arguments to solve probability questions.
- Employ additive and multiplicative structure of integers in number theory problems.
- Solve problems involving other number bases.
- Employ parity arguments and modular arithmetic in number theory problems.
- Understand various geometric terms and their definitions.
- Use angles, lengths, triangles, and areas to solve planar geometry problems.
- Solve basic 3D geometry problems.

## Prerequisites
Before starting this course, students should have completed Year 6 in the NZ mathematics curriculum, or Year 5 in the Australian mathematics curriculum. In particular, students should:
- Have strong computational skills with integers, decimals, fractions, and percentages.
- Understand order of mathematical operations, common mathematical symbols, and mathematical notation.
- Be able to solve linear equations in one variable.
- Have a basic understanding of the coordinate plane.
